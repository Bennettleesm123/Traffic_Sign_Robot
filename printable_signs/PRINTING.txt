Stopping the camera (release the webcam)
------------------------------------------
There is no separate “disconnect” command. The USB camera is released when the OpenCV
program exits:

  • In the preview window: press **q**
  • In the terminal: **Ctrl+C**

After that, the script calls cap.release() and you can unplug the camera or run another app.

Files in this folder (US MUTCD–style, public domain via MUTCD)
-------------------------------------------------------------
Open any .svg in a browser or Inkscape/Illustrator, then print at large size (A3 or US Letter
“fit to page” borderless if possible). Matte paper reduces glare for CV.

**Same print size as R3-5L / R3-5R (recommended for a consistent test set)**  
Use these so STOP and the turn arrows share one sheet geometry (easier for the camera):

  MUTCD_R1-1_stop_same_size_as_R3-5_sheet.svg   Stop (R1-1), scaled to R3-5 sheet proportions
  MUTCD_R3-5L_left_turn_only.svg                 Lane must turn left (R3-5L)
  MUTCD_R3-5R_right_turn_only.svg                Lane must turn right (R3-5R)

**U-turn (for training label → robot U-turn maneuver)**  
  MUTCD_R3-19a_u_turn_only.svg                   “Lane for U Turn Only” (R3-19a)

In `robot/sign_policy.py`, map your YOLO class name to `U_TURN` (defaults include:
`u turn`, `u-turn`, `u turn only`, `lane for u turn only`).

**Other arrows (optional)**  
  MUTCD_R1-1_stop.svg              Full-size stop R1-1 (30×30 in artwork; larger sheet than R3-5)
  MUTCD_R3-6L_left_arrow.svg       Left or straight (R3-6L)
  MUTCD_R3-6R_right_arrow.svg      Right or straight (R3-6R)

R3-5L / R3-5R are the clearest “only left” / “only right” arrows for training. R3-6L/R3-6R show
two movements on one sign (good for harder tests).

Traffic lights for the HSV stage
--------------------------------
This repo does not ship a traffic-light graphic; for printed tests use either:
  • A real USB traffic-light toy, or
  • A sheet with three large solid circles (red / yellow / green) side by side, or
  • Search Wikimedia Commons for “traffic light” diagrams (check license).

Match your YOLO class name for the *detector* to one of the strings in
robot/settings.py (traffic_light_generic_substrings). Color is handled by
robot/traffic_light_color.py on the crop.
