CV Rover Signs — training vs COCO (read this with CV_Rover_Signs.pdf in this folder)
===================================================================================

What the PDF shows (MUTCD-style regulatory signs for your rover):
  • Stop
  • Left turn only  (e.g. R3-5L)
  • Right turn only (e.g. R3-5R)
  • U-turn only     (e.g. R3-19a)
  • Traffic lights  (detector + optional HSV color stage in code)

FUNDAMENTAL LIMIT: yolov8n.pt (COCO-80) does NOT have classes for “left turn only”, “right turn only”,
or “U-turn only”. It only overlaps your PDF for:
  • stop sign
  • traffic light
  • (person — kept for your requirement)

So reliable detection of turn/U-turn signs REQUIRES a fine-tuned YOLO (or similar) trained on images
of those exact printed signs from your PDF.

Suggested training layout
-------------------------
  rover_signs_dataset/
    images/train/   *.jpg
    images/val/     *.jpg
    labels/train/   *.txt   # YOLO bbox format, one line per box: cls cx cy w h (normalized)
    labels/val/

Class indices must match ``names`` in your data.yaml. Example: training/rover_signs.yaml.example

Policy mapping
--------------
robot/sign_policy.py DEFAULT_LABEL_TO_ACTION must match the string values in ``names`` (e.g.
"left turn only", "u turn only"). Underscore aliases (left_turn_only, …) are also listed there.

After training, run inference with:
  python -m robot.camera_test --yolo path/to/best.pt --class-filter none

COCO demo with less clutter
---------------------------
For yolov8n.pt only, the code uses predict(classes=[0,9,11]) so chairs/laptops/etc. are never
proposed. This does not add missing sign classes.
